The MACE-MI registry


A cohort of 2704 patients who had chronic coronary heart disease.

They were followed for MACE events and all-cause mortality.

Measures:

  • The primary end point was time to MACE event.
  • MACE was defined as the incidence of any of the following events: cardiovascular mortality, non-fatal ACS, hemorrhagic or ischemic stroke, hospital readmission for heart failure, unplanned revascularization, acute ischemic events in lower extremities or new-onset atrial fibrillation.
  • Previous heart failure was codified if patients had at least one hospitalization with such main diagnosis at discharge-medical report as well as those with typical signs and symptoms of heart failure that had a compatible imagine diagnosis (X-ray or echocardiogram).
  • Glomerular filtration rate was estimated from serum creatinine levels by the Modification of Diet in Renal Disease equation.
  • Anemia was defined as hemoglobin ≤13 g/dl in males or ≤12 in females.

Outcomes:

  • 2704 patients were included. The median follow-up time was 14.9 months (interquartile range 12.2-70.1). By the end of that period, 455 patients (16.8%) developed MACE.

Statistical rationale:

  • The score is based in a Fine & Gray model for competing risks.
  • A competing risk (CR) is an event whose occurrence either precludes the occurrence of another event under examination or fundamentally alters the probability of occurrence of this other event.
  • Kaplan-Meier methods are not suitable for modeling CRs, as they tend to overestimate incidence.
  • Fine & Gray models have use in predicting an individualâ€™s incidence of an event (MACE), taking into account the possibility that a competing event (all-cause mortality) occurs.
  • The point-based score transforms the time-to-event model into a simple set of operations, to facilitate its use in the clinical setting.

Age   

  • ≤ 75: 0 points.
  • > 75: 3 points.

Gender   

  • Female: 0 points.
  • Male: 4 points.

Anemia   

  • No: 0 points.
  • Yes: 1 point.

Atrial Fibrillation   

  • No: 0 points.
  • Yes: 4 points.

Diabetes   

  • 2 points if diagnosed.

Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR)   

  • 2 points if GFR ≤60 ml/min/1.72m2.

Peripheral artery disease   

  • 4 points if diagnosed.

Heart Failure   

  • 5 points if diagnosed.
Abbreviations: MACE=major adverse cardiovascular events; GFR=glomerular filtration rate.
